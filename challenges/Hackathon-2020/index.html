Introduction

In collaboration with QED and the USAID Mission in Colombia, LASER will hold a data visualization hackathon during the Spring of 2020 that builds off of similar events conducted by USAID Colombia, and QED through the Center for Global Data Visualization. The Center for Global Data Visualization has experience holding hackathons, trainings and events in collaboration with Georgetown University, George Washington University, Addis Ababa University, Delhi University, Emory University and others. Similarly, in the last two years USAID Co-lombia has organized and held “datajams” on themes such as rural development and citizen security that resulted in creative and informa-tive data visualizations.

The LASER hackathon will be held with student groups across two Indiana campuses (Purdue, Notre Dame) and two or more universities in Colombia; the thematic subject of the data visualizations will be the Venzuelan Migration Crisis in Colombia. The USAID Colombia Mis-sion will provide guidance on the topical content and associated data, and will select the winning submissions. The event will be conducted principally remotely/online during a three week period (Feb.-March 2020), culminating with awards presented at the Purdue Shah Family Global Innovation Lab’s I2D Expo on April 1, 2020. Teams will be comprised of students from selected Colombian universities, as well as students from Purdue and Notre Dame. Each team of 3-4 persons will consist of a subject matter expert (or someone familiar with the context), a data scientist, and a designer. The specific objective of the hackathon is to engage students in contributing to international de-velopment solutions, through QED/LASER/USAID collaboration, with the result that relevant data visualizations are produced that are of use to the Colombia Mission.

Purpose and Scope

The overarching objective of the event is to help increase demand and utilization of data analytics at higher education institutions to in-crease efficiency and effectiveness in international development. The Hackathon will bring together teams of participants from multiple universities to demonstrate the power of open data for public good. Teams will assess, analyze and visualize data and present to an audi-ence to communicate a recommendation, story, or new idea through data visualization. This allows for better decision-making and more varied solutions to problems that span a wide range of public health topics.
