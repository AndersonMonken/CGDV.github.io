Introduction

In collaboration with QED and the USAID Mission in Colombia, LASER will hold a data visualization hackathon during the Spring of 2020 that builds off of similar events conducted by USAID Colombia, and QED through the Center for Global Data Visualization. The Center for Global Data Visualization has experience holding hackathons, trainings and events in collaboration with Georgetown University, George Washington University, Addis Ababa University, Delhi University, Emory University and others. Similarly, in the last two years USAID Co-lombia has organized and held “datajams” on themes such as rural development and citizen security that resulted in creative and informa-tive data visualizations. The LASER hackathon will be held with student groups across two Indiana campuses (Purdue, Notre Dame) and two or more universities in Colombia; the thematic subject of the data visualizations will be the Venzuelan Migration Crisis in Colombia. The USAID Colombia Mis-sion will provide guidance on the topical content and associated data, and will select the winning submissions. The event will be conducted principally remotely/online during a three week period (Feb.-March 2020), culminating with awards presented at the Purdue Shah Family Global Innovation Lab’s I2D Expo on April 1, 2020. Teams will be comprised of students from selected Colombian universities, as well as students from Purdue and Notre Dame. Each team of 3-4 persons will consist of a subject matter expert (or someone familiar with the context), a data scientist, and a designer. The specific objective of the hackathon is to engage students in contributing to international de-velopment solutions, through QED/LASER/USAID collaboration, with the result that relevant data visualizations are produced that are of use to the Colombia Mission.
The Hackathon will bring together teams of participants from across the world to demonstrate the power of open data for public good. Teams will assess, analyze and visualize data and present to an audience to communicate a recommendation, story, or new idea through data visualization. This allows for better decision-making and more varied solutions to problems that span a wide range of public health topics.

What is a Data Visualization Hackathon?

A data visualization hackathon is a design sprint-like event in which people collaborate intensively on data visualization projects.

Find Out More About Our Previous Hackathon at George Washington University

Who can participate?

Everyone!

What’s required?

A computer or laptop with your favorite tools installed. You can use whatever tools you like! (Excel, Adobe, Python, R, JavaScript, Tableau, Plotly, etc.)

Why should I participate?

In addition to the chance to earn prizes you’ll be able to develop a visualization that showcases a fresh perspective on data, opens up new feedback loops, informs policy, and improves program implementation for the public good!
