USAID HIV/AIDS' Road to 90-90-90

USAID’s HIV and AIDS program has now been on the forefront of the global AIDS crisis for 30 years. USAID, through PEPFAR, is working to achieve the Joint United Nations Programme on HIV/AIDS' ambitious 90–90–90 global goals by 2020.

USAID Antiretroviral (ARV) and HIV Lab Shipments to Supported Countries

In the beginning of 2008, total line item value of ARV by USAID to supported countries is $54M and HRDT is $11M. As per latest available numbers (Sep 2015), these numbers have considerably gone up and the total line item value of ARV by USAID to supporting countries is $1.4B and HRDT is $213M.

Note: ACT: Artemisinin-based Combination Therapy; ANTM: Anti-malarial medicine; ARV: Anti-Retroviral Treatment; HRDT: HIV Rapid Diagnostic Test; MRDT: Malarial Rapid Diagnostic Test



The biggest supplier of ARVs has been SCMS (Supply Chain Management System) established in 2005 to serve the PEPFAR programs and since 2006 has supplied ARVs worth over $1B to supported countries. Further drilling down the shipment value by supported countries, USAID has shipped over $350M of ARV and HIV lab supplies to Nigeria since 2006; closely followed by its shipment of ARV and HIV lab supplies of over $246M to Zambia during the same period. USAID’s focus on Nigeria is not only because it is one of the most populous country but also that its health statistics present a grim picture; it has the world’s second highest number of persons living with HIV/AIDS after South Africa.
