Coverage of Prior HIV Testing
in Uganda


As per 2017 estimates, there were about 1.3 million people living in Uganda with HIV/ AIDS, and an estimated 26,000 people died of AIDS-related illnesses. There are a far higher number of women than men affected with 8.8% of adult women living with HIV compared to 4.3% of men. Comparing 2011 and 2016, on awareness about where to get the HIV testing done, over 90% men and women know where to get themselves tested for HIV and there is a further increase in awareness in 2016 with 97% women and 95.6% men aware on where to get tested for HIV. Uganda has achieved remarkable improvement in increasing the percentage of people ever receiving HIV testing in 2016. Compared to 2011, where only Northern region had about 84.9% of women and 74.1% of men ever receiving HIV treatment; the highest across all the regions of Uganda. In 2016, this improved quite remarkably to at least 80% of women ever receiving HIV testing across all regions of Uganda. But there is a clear trend of fewer men being aware and even fewer having ever received HIV testing. Globally too less than half of men living with HIV are on treatment, compared to 60% of women indicating that men are not using services to prevent HIV or to test for HIV.
