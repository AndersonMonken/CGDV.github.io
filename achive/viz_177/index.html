Traditional ideas about the roles of girls and women restrict their contributions to in a nations’ growth. Women in Kenya are underrepresented in decision-making positions and also have less access to education, land, and employment. To assess women empowerment in Kenya; this visual looks at women’s participation in decision-making in Kenya. It is perhaps the most direct measure of women's empowerment since it examines women's participation in crucial decision-making processes. The higher the score, the greater the indication of gender equity in decision-making. 64.1% women in Eastern Kenya (ranked highest) reported having a final say in all of the decisions compared to only 41.8% women in Western Kenya (ranked lowest). In having a final say in their own health-care, 86.8% women in Central Kenya reported to have a final say with a rank of 1 whereas, North Eastern Kenya is ranked the lowest with only 63.3% women reported having a final say in their own health-care.

How to Read the Graph: The intersection of regions on the x-axis and numbers "ranks" on the y-axis depict the rank of 8 region's in Kenya for "percentage of women who have the final say"; as you hover your mouse on these intersection points you can look at the % of women have say in with specific decisions in that region and its current rank. The drop-down box in the legend allows you to compare ranks & percentage of women who have the final say in any of the decisions with other four distinct categories – 1) None of the Decisions, 2) Own Health Care, 3) Making Large Purchases and 4) Visits to Family, Relatives, Friends. E.g., For Nairobi, 59.7% women have the final say in any of the decisions and it has a rank of 2 amongst 8 regions but in terms of women who have the final say in own health care Nairobi has a percentage of 78.1% with a rank of 4 amongst 8.
