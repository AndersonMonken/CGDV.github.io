Kenya’s Exports of ‘Machinery and Parts’ Lags behind Imports by Huge Margin

Kenya with a population of 51 million (2018 estimates) is also one of the most developed countries in East Africa. Followed by agriculture, forestry and fishing; manufacturing is the second largest sector and represents around 11% of its GDP. A deep-dive into its exports and imports of machinery and parts starting Q1 2015, one observes an expected seasonal pattern in its imports of machinery and parts peaking in (Q1-Q3) followed by a decline in the last quarter. When calculated as a % of imports, Kenya’s exports of machinery and parts is in the range of 2-5% in this period. Highest value of Kenya’s imports of machinery and parts has been KES 86.4 Million shilling in Q1 2017 and that of exports has been KES 4.3 Million shilling in Q2 2016; trend being clearly indicative of a huge negative balance of trade here.

Data source: Kenya National Bereau of Statistics
