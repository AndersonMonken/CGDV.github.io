Only less than 1% of the world’s water is suitable for drinking and water related disease cause 3.4 million deaths each year. Egypt has been suffering from severe water scarcity in recent years. Uneven water distribution, misuse of water resources and inefficient irrigation techniques have played havoc with its water security. Egypt has only 20 cubic meters per person of internal renewable freshwater resources, and relies heavily on the Nile River for its main source of water; it is the backbone of Egypt’s industrial and agricultural sector and is the primary source of drinking water for the population. But with country’s rapidly growing population, climate change and poor garbage and pollution management, the river has become disposed of contaminants that are life-threatening.

This trend has been noticeable in the regions where from 2008 to 2014, all regions had a really high percentage of using untreated water for drinking with only less than 8% population using treated water. But in 2014, we see a much higher percentage relying on using treated water for drinking. This has also reflected in the % of households using an appropriate treatment method for drinking water going up from 3.3% in 2008 to 10.6% in 2014.
