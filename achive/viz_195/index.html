Ebola outbreak in DRC now declared a Public Health Emergency of International Concern



Ebola, a virus that causes severe bleeding, organ failure and can lead to death also known as Ebola virus disease (EVD) or, Ebola haemorrhagic fever (EHF) is an infectious disease. One can get EVD through direct contact with an infected animal (bat or nonhuman primate) or a sick or dead person infected with the virus. It has a high case fatality rate (CFR) with an average of about 50% and affects males and females equally.

On August 1, 2018, the Ministry of Health of the DRC reported an outbreak of Ebola virus disease in North Kivu Province. And since, the outbreak is continuing to spread in Eastern DRC with confirmed cases reported in 22 health zones of North Kivu and Ituri provinces. This is the 10th Ebola outbreak in DRC since the virus was discovered in 1976 in DRC.

As of July 31st, the highest number of deaths at 450 in Katwa, 377 in Beni, 300 in Butembo and, 278 in Mabalako have been reported; all four are North Kivu provinces. Mandima from Ituri also reported as many as 220 Ebola Cases. From the charts, one gets a sense that the both cases and no. of deaths really spiralled up in April. Complicating things, the local population has little trust in the authorities and their ability to respond. Since the start of the outbreak, there have been over 100 attacks on health workers and Ebola treatment centres. Health workers often move around with armed escorts, which arouses suspicion.
